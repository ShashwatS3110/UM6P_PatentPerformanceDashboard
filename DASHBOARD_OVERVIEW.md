# UM6P Patent Dashboard — What It Tells You (Plain-Language Guide)

*A two-page orientation for anyone opening the dashboard for the first time.*

---

## The one big question

Everything in this dashboard exists to answer a single question about UM6P's patents:

> **Are we building a patent portfolio that is high-quality, well-protected internationally, and turning into real value — not just a growing pile of paperwork?**

UM6P now holds **426 patents**. Counting them is easy. The harder, more important questions are: *Are they any good? Are they protected in the countries that matter? Are they making money?* The dashboard is organised to answer those, tab by tab.

---

## What each tab is really saying

Think of the tabs as a story that moves from **"how much"** to **"how good"** to **"so what."**

- **Overview** — The one-glance scorecard. A six-pointed "radar" compares UM6P against its neighbours (Morocco, Tunisia, South Africa) on quality and international reach. *Bigger shape = stronger.*
- **Scale & Momentum** — *Are we speeding up?* Yes: 2025 was a record year (25 filings) and filings have grown three years running, increasingly from UM6P's own labs rather than the older MAScIR portfolio.
- **Labs & Inventors** — *Who is creating the IP?* A small number of labs and inventors produce most of it — useful for spotting both strengths and key-person risk.
- **Research Themes** — *What are the patents about?* Heavily concentrated in environmental tech, mining and phosphate chemistry — UM6P's roots.
- **Global Coverage** — *Are we protected abroad?* Mostly **no**. The vast majority of patents are filed only in Morocco. This is the portfolio's biggest gap.
- **Portfolio Quality** — *Are the patents good compared to peers?* Roughly middle-of-the-pack regionally: decent originality and breadth, but low citation impact so far (partly because the patents are young).
- **Commercialization** — *Are patents making money?* Largely unknown today — this data hasn't been provided yet, so these tiles are placeholders.
- **Industrial Partnership** — *Who do we file with?* Most patents are filed alone; where there is an industry partner, it is almost always OCP.

## The honesty rule (read this)

Every tile carries a small label in its corner:

- **"Tracked now"** = a real number, computed from actual data.
- **"Illustrative"** = a **placeholder** shown only so the layout is complete. It is *not real* and will fill in automatically once the underlying data arrives.

So when you see "Illustrative," read it as *"this is where a real number will go."* Nothing on the dashboard is invented and passed off as fact.

One more nuance worth knowing: **volume, themes and coverage** cover the **full 426 patents**, but the **quality-vs-peers comparison** is based on a **small international sample (~28 patents)** — the only ones that show up in global quality databases. That comparison is a useful *direction*, not a final verdict.

---

## The five things to actually pay attention to

If you take away only a handful of points, make them these:

1. **International protection is the #1 gap.** Only ~8% of patents are filed beyond Morocco, versus ~12% for Morocco overall and ~37% for South Africa. A patent that only exists in Morocco cannot be enforced anywhere its technology would actually be sold.

2. **Momentum is real and in-house.** Filings are at a record and increasingly come from UM6P's own labs — the pipeline is genuinely accelerating, not a blip.

3. **Quality is average, not a standout — yet.** UM6P sits mid-pack among regional peers on originality and breadth, and low on citation impact. Much of that is because the patents are young (citations take 5–7 years to build), so this is a "watch this space" rather than a red flag.

4. **The portfolio is aging and concentrated.** Active patents have ~10 years of protection left on average, a handful expire within 5 years, and there are ~72 old, Morocco-only patents still being paid for. These are decisions waiting to be made: commercialise, extend, or let lapse.

5. **We depend on one partner and one funnel narrows sharply.** Industry collaboration runs almost entirely through OCP, and very few patents reach a commercial licence. Broadening partners and working the existing portfolio matter more than simply filing more.

## The questions to ask in the room

- Which of our best patents should we **file internationally now**, before deadlines pass?
- Which old, domestic-only patents should we **stop paying for**?
- How do we **convert solo filings into industry collaborations** that lead to revenue?
- What data do we still need to make the **commercialization** picture real?

*The dashboard is designed to make each of these a click away: click any tile with a magnifying-glass icon to see the actual patents behind the number.*
