Methods & Sources
How every number on this dashboard is calculated, and where the data comes from.

What this dashboard answers

Whether UM6P is building a patent portfolio that is high-quality, well-protected internationally, and generating economic value.

How to read it — two things to know

  • Every tile is badged. Tracked now = computed from real data. Illustrative = a labelled placeholder shown so the layout is complete; it becomes real automatically once its data source is added. Nothing invented is presented as verified.
  • Two lenses, kept separate. Volume, themes, coverage, labs and collaboration use the full 426-patent inventory. Quality and peer benchmarking use an internationally-tracked subset (~28 UM6P patents) — the only ones that appear in international quality databases — and are directional, not a full-portfolio verdict.

The radar compares six quality/impact metrics for UM6P vs a selected regional peer, each axis normalised so the strongest of the four countries reaches 100. Source: national patent-quality datasets.

Scale & Momentum

  • Patent Filings — count of applications per year, split by originating institution. Master inventory.
  • Annual Filing Growth — 3-year compound annual growth rate of dated filings (2022→2025), with the year-by-year trend. Master inventory.
  • Time to Patent Grant — average of (grant date − filing date) over patents with both dates. MAScIR renewal registry.
  • Idea-to-Revenue funnel — filed and granted stages are real (application count; granted/maintained status); disclosure and licensing stages are illustrative.

Labs & Inventors

  • Patents by Lab, by status — count per originating lab/hub, split into granted-active / pending / lapsed-rejected / not-recorded. Master inventory.
  • Most Active Inventors / Top Researchers — patents per named inventor (co-inventors split), with their main lab and domain. Master inventory.

Research Themes

  • Theme distribution & momentum — patent counts per theme, overall and per year. Master inventory — UM6P's own theme tags.
  • Portfolio vs Strategy — each theme's share minus a target weight. The target weights are placeholder assumptions pending UM6P's declared R&D allocation.
  • Forward Citations by Theme — total forward citations per theme across the international citation sample (~25 patents). Citation export.

Global Coverage

  • Filing Countries — each application counted once by its primary filing route; a missing country is shown as a data gap, not assumed domestic. Master inventory.
  • International Filing Rate — share of applications filed beyond Morocco (PCT/foreign). Master inventory.
  • Top-Market (Triadic) Coverage — share of families protected in Europe + US + Japan together. Patent-family data.
  • Portfolio Survival Rate — share of the renewal registry still paying annuities. MAScIR renewal registry.
  • Average Remaining Patent Life — 20 years minus current age, averaged over active patents with a known filing date. Master inventory.

Portfolio Quality (international subset)

  • High-Impact (top-10% cited), Originality, Technology Scope, Science Linkage — standard patent-quality indicators for UM6P vs the Morocco / Tunisia / South Africa averages, patent-weighted, on the international subset. National patent-quality datasets.
  • Forward Citations per patent — average forward citations across ~25 internationally-indexed patents. Citation export.

Commercialization

  • Pruning Candidates — patents 10+ years old, protected only in Morocco, still maintained. Master inventory.
  • Commercialization rate, deals, revenue — illustrative, pending a TTO licensing/deal registry.

Industrial Partnership & Ventures

  • Industry Concentration (HHI) — Herfindahl-Hirschman index of co-filing partners (0 = diversified, 1 = one partner). Master inventory partner fields.
  • Industry–Academia Collaboration — every patent classified as filed independently, with an industry partner, or with another university. Master inventory.
  • Spin-offs & patent-to-startup — illustrative, pending a venture registry.

Data sources

  • UM6P patent master inventory — the consolidated TTO/OMPIC record of all 426 patents.
  • MAScIR renewal registry — filing/grant dates and annuity status for the legacy portfolio.
  • Espacenet patent-family data — international family members, technology classes, jurisdictions.
  • Orbit citation export — forward citations for the internationally-indexed subset.
  • WIPO PCT records — international filing / national-phase data.
  • National patent-quality datasets (Morocco, Tunisia, South Africa) — OECD-style patent-quality indicators used for peer benchmarking.

Caveats & limitations

  • ~127 patents have no filing country recorded, ~69 no legal status, ~35 no filing year — so the grant rate is a conservative floor and coverage is understated.
  • Grant, survival and time-to-grant come from the MAScIR renewal registry only, not the full portfolio.
  • Quality/peer figures rest on a small international sample (~28 patents) and effectively stop around 2023.
  • Themes are UM6P's own single tag per patent; cross-cutting inventions sit in one bucket.
  • OCP concentration may be over-stated — framework-reference tags are counted as OCP-linked.
  • Peer benchmarks currently exist for quality metrics only.

Reproducibility

Every "Tracked now" figure is produced by a published, re-runnable script directly from the source files above — replace a data file and re-run to update the whole dashboard. Corrections and questions: open an issue.

Glossary & Metric Dictionary
Every term and metric on the dashboard, in plain language.
General terms
  • Tracked now / Illustrative — a real computed figure vs. a labelled placeholder awaiting data.
  • Internationally-tracked subset — the ~28 UM6P patents that appear in international patent databases (used for the quality comparison); the other lens is the full 426-patent inventory.
  • Forward citations — how often later patents cite this one; the standard proxy for technological influence.
  • Annuity / renewal — the recurring fee to keep a granted patent in force; patents lapse if unpaid.
  • PCT — the international treaty route for filing one application across many countries at once.
Overview
  • Quality & Impact radar — six quality/impact measures for UM6P vs a selected regional peer; each axis normalised so the strongest of the four countries reaches 100.
Scale & Momentum
  • Patent Filings — new applications filed per year, split by originating institution.
  • Annual Filing Growth (CAGR) — compound annual growth rate of filings over the last three years.
  • Patent Output per Researcher — applications filed per 100 active researchers.
  • Time to Patent Grant — average years from filing to the office granting it.
  • Annual Invention Disclosures — ideas submitted to the TTO before any filing (top of the pipeline).
  • Idea-to-Revenue Funnel — cumulative counts at each stage: projects → disclosures → filings → grants → licences.
Labs & Inventors
  • Patents by Lab / Hub — patents per originating lab, split by legal status.
  • Most Active Inventors / PIs — inventors credited on the most patents, with their main lab.
  • Top Researchers by Output — the same ranking with each researcher's lab and main domain.
Research Themes
  • Patent Portfolio by Research Theme — share of the portfolio in each of UM6P's five themes.
  • Research Theme Momentum — filings per theme per year (direction of travel).
  • Portfolio vs Declared Strategy — each theme's share minus its target weight (targets are placeholders).
  • Commercial Success by Theme — share of each theme's patents on a commercial path (illustrative).
  • Forward Citations by Theme — total forward citations landing in each theme, across the international sample.
Global Coverage
  • Patent Filing Countries — where each application is protected, counted once by primary route.
  • International Filing Rate — share of applications filed beyond Morocco.
  • Top-Market (Triadic) Coverage — share protected in Europe + US + Japan simultaneously.
  • Patent Portfolio Survival Rate — share of the renewal registry still paying annuities.
  • Average Remaining Patent Life — average years of protection left before the 20-year expiry.
Portfolio Quality (international subset)
  • High-Impact Patents — share ranking in the top 10% most-cited for their field and cohort.
  • Originality — how broadly a patent draws on diverse technology fields (0–1).
  • Technology Scope — how many distinct technology fields a patent spans.
  • Science Linkage (NPL) — how directly a patent cites scientific (non-patent) literature.
  • Forward Citations per Patent — average forward citations across the international sample.
Commercialization
  • Active Commercialization Rate — share of patents on a live commercial path (illustrative).
  • Licensing Agreements Signed — licence/option deals per year (illustrative).
  • Annual IP Revenue — royalties and equity income from IP (illustrative).
  • Revenue per Active Patent — IP income divided by active patents (illustrative).
  • Portfolio Pruning Candidates — old, domestic-only patents still being maintained (review-to-lapse).
Industrial Partnership & Ventures
  • Industry Concentration Index (HHI) — how concentrated co-filing is among partners (0 = diversified, 1 = one partner).
  • Industry–Academia Collaboration — every patent classified as filed independently, with an industry partner, or with another university.
  • Spin-off Companies Created — new companies founded on UM6P patents (illustrative).
  • Patent-to-Startup Conversion — share of patents licensed to a new startup rather than an established firm (illustrative).
AI Transparency Note
How this dashboard was built, and how accuracy is protected.

This dashboard's data pipeline, metric calculations, and interface were built with AI assistance (Claude, by Anthropic), working from a project brief and the editorial and analytical decisions set by the author.

How accuracy is protected

  • Every "Tracked now" figure is computed by a published, re-runnable script directly from the source data — not hand-entered — so any number can be traced back and reproduced.
  • Illustrative placeholders are clearly labelled and are never presented as verified data.
  • Data-source assumptions and edge cases (missing statuses, blank filing countries, partner-name variants) are handled explicitly and disclosed in Methods & Sources.
  • Peer benchmarks use standardized national patent-quality datasets and are scoped, in writing, to the internationally-tracked subset.

About the written insights

The short commentary beside each metric is AI-generated interpretation of the current data. The figures inside it update automatically whenever the data is refreshed, and the interpretation itself should be re-reviewed when the data changes materially. Treat it as analytical guidance to speed reading — not an official UM6P position.

AI accelerated the building; the framing, interpretation, and every judgment about what to show and how to caveat it are the author's. Errors are the author's responsibility — corrections are welcome via the repository.
